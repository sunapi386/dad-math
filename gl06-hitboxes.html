Game Lab · Topic 06

Aiming & Hitboxes

In Deep Rock Galactic you shoot a glyphid swarming out of the dark and — ping! — an orange number pops off its glowing back. How does the game know your bullet hit the bug at all, and how does it know you nailed the weak spot? The whole trick is an invisible shape and one line of math you already have.

⏱ ~30 min   friends with 🐦 GL·5 arcs

1 · The bug you see vs. the shape the game checks

The glyphid on screen is a gorgeous mess of legs, spikes and mandibles — way too complicated to ask "did the bullet touch any pixel of that?" a hundred times a second. So games cheat with a hitbox: a simple invisible shape — usually just a circle or a box — that stands in for the monster. You never see it, but the game only ever checks the shape.

Deep Rock adds a second, smaller shape over the squishy bits — the glowing weak point. Land your shot inside that little circle and it counts for extra damage. Two circles, that's the whole secret: a big one for "hit the bug", a small one for "hit the weak spot".

2 · The shooting range

🎯 Practice range

Move your aim over the bug and click / tap to shoot. Flip Show hitboxes to reveal the invisible circles the game is really testing. The glowing spot is the weak point — worth 3×.

Total damage: 0 — aim for the glowing weak spot!

3 · One line of math: is the shot inside?

A round hitbox is just a centre \((c_x, c_y)\) and a radius \(r\). Your shot lands at some point \((x, y)\). "Did I hit?" is exactly "is my point closer to the centre than the radius?" — the distance idea again:

$$(x - c_x)^2 + (y - c_y)^2 \;\le\; r^2 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \textbf{hit!}$$

The game checks the small weak-point circle first (bonus damage), then the big body circle, then calls it a miss. That's all "hit detection" is. (We square both sides so the computer never has to do a slow square root — a neat speed trick.)

Two kinds of gun: the range above is hitscan — the shot is an instant ray, so where you click is where it checks, right now (Scout's rifle, the Minigun). Other weapons fire a projectile that travels and arcs — the grenade launcher, the PGL — and those follow the exact parabola from GL·5. Same hitbox test, but you must lead the target and mind the drop.

4 · 📓 Homework check: weak-point challenge

This is the check a grown-up can assign. No lucky guessing — you have to actually aim: land 5 weak-point hits on the moving bug. Passing marks this lesson ✅ (worth ⭐⭐) and earns the 🐛 Bug Buster badge.

📓 Homework (on paper / out loud)

  1. Spot the hitbox. Next time you play, aim just past the edge of a bug and shoot. Sometimes it still hits, sometimes it whiffs on a leg. Where do you think the invisible circle really sits — tight on the body, or a bit bigger?
  2. Be the computer. A bug's body circle is at centre (5, 5) with radius 3. You shoot at (7, 6). Work out \((7-5)^2 + (6-5)^2\) and compare to \(3^2 = 9\). Hit or miss?
  3. Hitscan or projectile? List two Deep Rock weapons that hit instantly and two that lob a travelling shot. Which kind needs you to aim ahead of a running bug, and why?
