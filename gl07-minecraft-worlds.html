Minecraft Worlds

In Random Worlds you grew caves with a drunk digger. But Minecraft's overworld isn't a scribble — it's hills: smooth, rolling, infinite in every direction, dressed in biomes… and if you walk a thousand chunks away and come back, your house is exactly where you left it, even though none of it is saved anywhere. Today we build that actual trick.

⏱ ~30 min   plays well after GL·4   🧱 you'll walk an infinite world

1 · Why pure randomness makes TV static, not hills

2 · The in-betweening rule (the one formula today)

Between an anchor of height a and the next of height b, how tall is the ground part-way across? Slide a fraction t from 0 to 1 and mix:

$$h \;=\; a + (b - a)\,t$$

At t = 0 you get a. At t = 1 you get b. Halfway (t = ½) you get the average. That's called linear interpolation — “lerp” — and it's everywhere in games: terrain, animation blending, camera moves, colour fades. (Minecraft-style noise adds one extra touch: it eases t along a curve so hilltops come out rounded, not pointy — that's the smoothing we use below.)

3 · Infinite world, zero storage: chunks

Here's the beautiful part. The world is sliced into chunks (Minecraft uses 16-block slices). A chunk's terrain is computed by a recipe that eats only two numbers: the world seed and the chunk number. Nothing is stored — chunk 7,203 doesn't exist until you walk toward it, and it's rebuilt from the recipe every single time. Same seed, same chunk number → same answer, forever. That's why your house is always there, and why the world can be bigger than every hard drive on Earth.

4 · Biomes: a second recipe on the same seed

Deserts, plains, snow — Minecraft decides them with another noise, a slow temperature wave across the world. It uses the same seed mixed with a different flavour number — exactly the salt trick from the logins lesson! One seed, many independent recipes: one for height, one for temperature, one for ore pockets, one for trees. Cold stretch → ❄️ snow; middle → 🌳 plains with trees; hot → 🌵 desert sand. You can see the bands change as you walk the explorer.

📓 Homework

  1. Find your seed. In your Minecraft world, type /seed. Write it down. That number IS your entire world — every mountain, cave and village folds out of it.
  2. Prove the recipe. Create a new world with that same seed. Walk to where your base is in the original — is the terrain identical? (Your buildings won't be there. Why not? What's recipe, and what's saved?)
  3. Design a biome rule. Our recipe uses one temperature wave. Real Minecraft also uses a rainfall wave. Invent the rule: hot + wet = ? hot + dry = ? cold + wet = ?
