The Damage Budget

The 💣 Mortar hits for 14. The 🔫 Gunner hits for 3. So the Mortar is better… right? Wrong — and the reason is the most useful arithmetic in all of gaming. Tonight: damage per second, time-to-kill, wasted overkill, and the shopkeeper's question — what does one coin actually buy?

⏱ ~30 min   rates, ceilings, value   ⚔️ referee the duels

1 · Damage per second

A single hit number lies to you, because it hides time. The honest number multiplies in the firing rate:

$$\text{DPS} = \text{damage per shot} \times \text{shots per second}$$

Here are the real cards from the game you played — with the DPS the shop screen quietly showed you all along:

Surprise: the mighty Mortar (7 DPS) barely beats the humble Gunner (6 DPS) — it just bunches its damage into rare, huge packets. And the Cryo is a joke at 1 DPS… hold that thought for §4.

2 · Time-to-kill, and the wasted bite

A wave-5 grunt has 34 HP. How long does a Gunner (3 dmg, 2 shots/s) need? First: how many shots? 34 ÷ 3 = 11.33… and you can't fire a third of a bullet, so round up:

$$\text{shots} = \left\lceil \frac{\text{HP}}{\text{dmg}} \right\rceil = \lceil 34/3 \rceil = 12$$

Those 12 shots deal 36 damage to a 34 HP bug — 2 damage wasted. That waste is called overkill, and big-hit turrets suffer it worst: a Mortar (14 dmg) killing a 15 HP bug spends 28 damage — nearly half thrown away. Second: how long? The first shot fires the instant the bug enters the ring — it's free! — and then you wait between shots. With 12 shots at 2 per second: 11 waits × 0.5 s = 5.5 seconds.

$$\text{kill time} = \frac{\text{shots} - 1}{\text{rate}}$$

(Count the gaps, not the shots — the same fencepost trick as trees along a road.)

3 · The escape window

Here's where TD·1 crashes back in. A bug isn't in range forever — it walks a chord across the ring and leaves. Road offset b from the turret, range r: half the chord is a right-triangle leg again, so window length = 2\sqrt{r^2-b^2}. The whole duel is one comparison: kill time ≤ window time?

4 · What does one coin buy?

Now be the shopkeeper. Divide the DPS by the price:

🔫
Gunner
6 DPS ÷ 20💰0.30/coin
💣
Mortar
7 DPS ÷ 45💰0.16/coin
❄️
Cryo
1 DPS ÷ 30💰0.03/coin

Per coin, the Gunner is nearly twice the Mortar and ten times the Cryo. So why do the other two exist? Because raw DPS isn't the whole story: the Mortar's shell splashes — hit a clump of three bugs and it's really dealing 21 DPS. And the Cryo doesn't sell damage at all — it sells time: a chilled bug walks at half speed, so it spends twice as long inside every other turret's window. The Cryo doubles your other turrets. Judging helpers by their own damage is how shoppers get fooled — a lesson that works outside games too.
