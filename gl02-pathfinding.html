How Units Find Their Way

You click a far-off spot in StarCraft and your marine strolls around the buildings to get there. Nobody told it the route — it worked it out. The trick is beautiful and simple: the map floods, like water spilled at the start, and the first drop to reach the goal came the shortest way.

⏱ ~30 min   friends with 🕸️ Seven Bridges

1 · Spilled water finds the way

Imagine pouring water on the start square. In one second it seeps to every neighbour. Another second — their neighbours. It spreads in rings: everything 1 step away, then 2 steps, then 3… Walls block it, so the water bends around them. The instant the flood touches the goal, you're done — and because it grew one ring at a time, that had to be the shortest way in.

Computer scientists call this breadth-first search — “breadth first” because you finish a whole ring before starting the next. It's the same dots-and-lines world as the Seven Bridges: squares are dots, neighbours are lines.

2 · Watch it flood

🧭 The pathfinder

🟩 start   🟥 goal. Click or drag on the grid to build/erase walls (your “buildings”). Then press Find the path and watch the flood spread out and bend around them. The gold trail is the shortest route.

Build some walls, then find the path.
Try: wall the goal off completely — the flood fills the whole open area and never reaches it. That “no path” answer is exactly why a StarCraft unit sometimes just shrugs and stays put. Then leave one gap and watch the water squeeze through it.

3 · Why it's always the shortest

On an open grid with no walls, the shortest trip from here to there is just: steps across + steps up-or-down. No diagonal shortcuts allowed, so you can't do better than covering each on its own. Mathematicians call that the Manhattan distance — like a taxi in a city of square blocks.

$$\text{shortest steps} = |\Delta \text{col}| + |\Delta \text{row}|$$

The flood discovers this on its own: since it grows one ring per second, the ring number where it first hits the goal is that distance. Walls only ever make the answer bigger, never smaller.

4 · 🧩 Puzzle: count the steps

Open grid, no walls. How many steps is the shortest path from 🟩 to 🟥? Streak of 3 = ⭐, streak of 5 = 🧭 badge.

📓 Homework

  1. Play detective in a game. In Deep Rock or StarCraft, watch a character walk to a far spot. Did it go straight, or bend around something? What was it avoiding?
  2. Be the flood. On graph paper, mark a start and goal with a wall between them. Write 0 on the start, then 1 on its neighbours, 2 on theirs… until you reach the goal. The number you write there is the shortest distance.
  3. Think ahead: real games use a smarter cousin called A* that aims the flood toward the goal instead of spreading everywhere. Why would aiming save the computer work?
