🌳 The Builder's Tree

This is a game-development course shaped like a tech tree — the same way Deep Rock upgrades gear and Factorio unlocks research. Every node is one real game-making idea. Learn it in its lesson, pass its 3-question principles test, and that idea gets installed into your game below. Your game starts as nothing and grows, unlock by unlock, into a full cave shooter — and every piece of it is something you understand.

2 · 🌳 The research tree

Two branches grow from the loop — the combat branch (hitboxes → arcs) and the world branch (pathfinding → random worlds) — and they join at the sentry, which needs both. A node unlocks for testing when everything it points from is done. Read the lesson first; the test asks why, not just what, and one wrong answer ends the attempt.

🧪 Principles test

📓 How this mirrors real game development

  1. Engines are trees too. Unity, Unreal, Godot — all of them are these same six ideas (plus decoration) layered in this same order. Name which node each Deep Rock feature hangs off: bug swarms? sentry turrets? fresh caves every mission?
  2. Design your seventh node. If you could research one more mechanic for Crystal Caves, what would it be — and which existing nodes would it need? (Sound? Saving? Two players? Every one of those is a real branch on a real engine's tree.)
  3. Ship it. Show someone your game before the tree is finished. Real studios do exactly that — it's called a playtest, and half of game design is watching someone else play your unfinished thing.
