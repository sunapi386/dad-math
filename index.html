How a lesson works

Each session follows the same rhythm — the same one a mathematician uses:

① Start with a real question

“How would you add every number from 1 to 100?” Small enough to try, annoying enough to want a shortcut.

② Play with examples

Try n = 6, then n = 8. Notice a pattern. Say it out loud.

③ Make a hypothesis \therefore

Write your guess down with three dots. Then test it on a fresh example.

④ Generalize & box it □

Turn the pattern into a formula that works for any number. Draw a little square to mark “done.”

For grown-ups: read a section aloud, then hand the device to your kid and let them move the slider and make the guess. The goal isn't speed — it's the moment they say “wait, it's always…”.
