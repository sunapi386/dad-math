Factory Logic

Factorio looks like a game about conveyor belts. It's actually a game about ratios and logic — the exact two things a real computer chip is made of. People have built working calculators inside Factorio. Today, so will you: first balance a factory, then wire up a machine that adds.

⏱ ~30 min   pairs great with 💻 CS·1 Binary

1 · The secret is ratios

Every Factorio player learns this the hard way: if your makers pump out plates faster than your users can eat them, the belt clogs; too slow, and the users sit idle and starving. A smooth factory is balanced — supply equals demand.

Say each 🔥 furnace makes 2 plates/second and each 🏭 assembler needs 3 plates/second. Drag the counts until the two bars match. The magic balance is the smallest whole-number ratio.

2 · Switches that think: logic gates

Deeper in Factorio are circuit wires and combinators — parts that take switches (on/off, just like binary bits) and combine them by a rule. These rules are called logic gates, and there are only a few:

  • AND — on only if both inputs are on. (“Fire the alarm if the door is open AND it's night.”)
  • OR — on if either is on.
  • NOT — flips it: on becomes off.
  • XOR — on if the inputs disagree (exactly one on).

3 · 🧠 Build a machine that adds

Here's the payoff. To add two single bits you need to know two things: the sum digit and whether you carry a 1. Watch: 1+1 = 2, which in binary is 10 — a 0 with a carried 1. It turns out:

$$\text{sum} = a \mathbin{\text{XOR}} b \qquad\qquad \text{carry} = a \mathbin{\text{AND}} b$$

That's it. Two gates you already met, wired side by side, and you've built a half adder — the seed of every calculator, phone, and console ever made. Flip the inputs and check all four cases:

🧠 The half adder
+ = 00
Flip the bits — try 1 + 1.
You did it: 1 + 1 lights carry = 1, sum = 0 → binary 10 = two. Chain a few of these together (a “full adder”) and you can add any two numbers at all. That chain, millions of times over, is the chip running the game you're playing.

📓 Homework

  1. Spot a ratio in a game. Next Factorio session (or Lego build), find one place where you need “N of these to feed M of those.” Write the smallest whole-number ratio.
  2. Talk in gates. Turn a house rule into a gate: “I get dessert if I finished dinner AND my room is clean.” Which gate? What about “…dinner OR chores”?
  3. Add by hand: use the sum = XOR, carry = AND rule to add the bits of 1 + 1, then 1 + 0. Does it match binary from CS·1?
